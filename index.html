My name is Despina. Most people call me Des, but it all depends on personal preference, as long as you pronounce Des with an S and not a Z. I’m a 21 year-old student who lives in Boston, Massachusetts and I study communications, media studies, and graphic design. This webpage is an online version of a magazine I created called ZAMANFOU . This term is Greek slang and is derived from the French phrase, Je m’en fous which means a lack of caring. A mental state of IDGAF. I don’t care, I am not interested; I’m Zamanfou.

With that being said, I’ve curated this special edition of the magazine to focus primarily on the elderly population and ageism. Being so young, many might say that I still have a lot to learn, see, and experience in my life. I would say that’s an understatement. I would also say that one of the best ways to do this is by communicating with those who have been around the longest. In other words, talk to some old people. They’re the ones who know everything, and they’ve been here for what feels like forever. Since they are at an older age, they can pretty much do and say whatever they want without caring, without consequences and without almost anyone ever talking back. We’ve been taught to respect our elders am I right? This means they can walk, talk, and dress how they want. In other words they’re cool as hell. Kings and queens.

I believe that the older generation should always be cherished and respected. They have paved the way for us younger people and have started revolutions. It’s now our job to keep them going. Of course we have those people who take it to a new level and say very inappropriate things, but in their heads I’m sure they are just Zamanfou. Who cares anyways? I might still have much to learn, but one thing I know is that life is short. Wear what you want, say what you want, always stand up for what you believe in. Do what you love, love who you do, and always with pathos. Age is just a number and it should never be a factor in making decisions that can lead to a result of pure joy and happiness. Sometimes you just gotta say, Zamanfou.
